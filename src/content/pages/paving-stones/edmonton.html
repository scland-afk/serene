Paving Stones in Edmonton

Durable patios, walkways, and driveways built with proper base work, compaction, and jointing so your hardscape stays level and drains for years.

We install interlocking pavers and patio stones with excavation, a solid base, and edge restraint so surfaces stay level and drain properly. Our crews focus on base work and jointing that hold up over time. We do not cut corners on excavation depth, compaction, or polymeric sand, because that is what prevents heaving and settling. We serve residential and commercial properties across the Edmonton area, including St. Albert, Spruce Grove, and Sherwood Park.

What Paving Stone Installation Means Here

Proper installation starts below the surface. Our process includes:

  • Excavation to the correct depth for base and pavers
  • Base layer of crushed aggregate, properly compacted
  • Slope for drainage away from structures
  • Edge restraint so pavers do not shift over time
  • Polymeric sand in joints for stability and weed resistance
  • Drainage considered around downspouts and low spots

When base and compaction are done right, your paver patio or walkway stays tight and level for years.

Applications We Install

We install paving stones for a range of uses:

Patios. Outdoor living areas that connect to your home or yard. We size and slope patios for furniture and drainage so water does not pool.

Walkways. Paths from driveway to door, through gardens, or between outdoor zones. Walkways get the same base and edge restraint as patios for consistency.

Driveways. Paver driveways require a deeper base and heavier compaction to support vehicles. We build driveways to handle daily use.

Steps and transitions. Steps, landings, and transitions from patio to lawn are integrated into the design so levels and drainage work together.

Types of Pavers We Install

We work with concrete pavers, brick pavers, natural stone, permeable pavers, and large-format slabs. The choice depends on your design, budget, and how the area will be used. We focus on installation quality: proper base, compaction, and jointing so whatever material you choose performs well.

Benefits of a Proper Installation

  • Long-term performance. A well-built base and jointing reduce heaving and shifting over time.
  • Repairability. Individual pavers can be lifted and replaced if one is damaged, unlike poured concrete.
  • Traction. Textured pavers provide grip in rain and light snow.
  • Long-term value. Proper installation extends the life of the surface and avoids costly repairs.
  • Drainage control. Slope and base design direct water away from foundations and low spots.

Our Process

  1. Site visit. We look at access, drainage, existing grades, and how you plan to use the space.
  2. Design and measurements. We confirm layout, dimensions, and slope so the build matches the plan.
  3. Excavation and base. We excavate to depth, install and compact base material, and set edge restraint.
  4. Install. Pavers go in according to the layout, with cuts and transitions done cleanly.
  5. Final compaction and cleanup. We compact the surface, sweep in polymeric sand, and clean up the site.

Pricing Factors

Cost depends on access, excavation depth, base thickness, removal of existing surfaces, layout complexity, edges and steps, and any drainage work. We provide a clear quote after a site visit. We do not use fake numbers; your estimate reflects your specific project.
