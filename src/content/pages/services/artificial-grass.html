Proper Base Prep and Drainage

A solid base is the difference between turf that lasts and turf that fails early. We excavate 3-4", lay 2-3" of crusite or limestone screenings, and compact it flat with a plate compactor. Turf gets secured with 6" galvanized nails every 4-6" along edges and seams.

Turf Options and Infill Choices

We install turf with realistic color blends and blade heights that suit how the space is used. Shorter pile works well for high-traffic areas and pet runs, while taller blades give a softer look for full backyards. Seams are aligned so the grain runs the same direction and the finish stays consistent across the yard.

Infill helps the blades stand up and adds weight to keep the turf stable. We typically use clean silica sand for a natural feel and solid drainage. If the area needs extra stability, we can add heavier infill based on the site and use.

What to Expect After Installation

Once turf is installed, you can use the space right away. We recommend light brushing to keep the blades upright, rinsing pet areas as needed, and removing leaves or debris so drainage stays clear. We also walk the site with you to review seams, edges, and proper care.
