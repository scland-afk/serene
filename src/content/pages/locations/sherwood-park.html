Final Grading in Sherwood Park (Strathcona County)

For new residential lots (single dwelling, semi-detached, and townhouse), Strathcona County requires grading to match the approved surface drainage plan through review of a Lot Grading Certificate.

Rough Grade is typically handled by the builder. Final Grade is the homeowner's responsibility and must be initiated within one year of rough grade approval. If that deadline is missed, fines can start at $300.

Final grade targets generally include elevations within 5 cm (2 in) of design grades (not higher), property-line slope at a minimum of 2% with no sections below 1.5%, and clear side-yard swales with at least 300 mm of unobstructed width.
