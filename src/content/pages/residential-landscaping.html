How Much Does Residential Landscaping Cost in Edmonton?

Residential landscaping costs vary widely based on project scope, materials, and property size. Here are rough estimates for common projects in Edmonton:

  • Lawn Maintenance: $50-80 per visit (weekly or bi-weekly mowing, edging, trimming)
  • Spring/Fall Cleanup: $200-600 (depends on property size and debris removal)
  • Sod Installation: $0.70-0.99 per square foot (includes grading, topsoil, sod, installation)
  • Patio Installation: $35-45 per square foot (interlocking pavers, excavation, base, installation)
  • Complete Landscape Design: $5,000-50,000+ (varies greatly based on scope and features)
  • Final Grading: $1,500-3,500 (new construction properties, includes topsoil and grading)

The best way to get accurate pricing is to request a free quote. We'll visit your property and provide a detailed estimate based on your specific needs.

When Is the Best Time to Start a Residential Landscaping Project?

Spring (April-May): Ideal for landscape design, sod installation, garden bed creation, and drainage work. Demand is high, so book early.

Summer (June-August): Good for patios, hardscaping, and ongoing maintenance. Plants installed in summer need extra watering to establish roots.

Fall (September-October): Excellent for sod installation (cooler weather helps establishment), final grading, and preparing properties for winter.

Winter (November-March): Not ideal for most landscaping work, but we offer residential snow removal to keep driveways and walkways clear.

For the best selection of dates and materials, we recommend booking spring projects in February or March. Summer and fall projects can often be scheduled 2-4 weeks in advance.
